The Super Sketchy Mario/Seriously Sketchy Material Website

What is it about?

This website will have Super Sketchy Mario comics and other art that I have posted. Ideally, I would like to have every comic and all art (including drawings posted exclusively on twitter), but, considering there are over 200 Super Sketchy Mario comics, I probably won't have them all on here for a while.

Why is it important?

This website is meant to bring all of my projects under SSM together into one place. Before this site, if you wanted to see everything I have made you would have to go to two Deviantart pages and a twitter page, but now it's all on this one website with a much simpler way to navigate between the comics and the art.

Who is this for?

This website is mainly for fans of my work who want to browse everything that I have made in a more convenient way. Since SSM has been and always will be a passion project, this page is not necessarily meant to draw in new readers. As for SSM itself, it's targeted towards fans of video games. Mario and other Nintendo properties are the main focus, but I do branch out into other games like Toontown, A Hat in Time, and Team Fortress 2. Additionally, I do like to make my own stories, characters, and worlds, but that's not really what people read Super Sketchy Mario for.

What is going to be on each page?

Homepage: This page will have the logo and buttons that go to the comic and art directories and the feedback page. It will have some quick links to different comics and art like the latest comic, latest drawing, Friendart of the Month, and the first comic so people can start reading from the beginning. I'd also like to include a poll somewhere on the page for some comic-related feedback. Questions would be along the lines of "Who is your favorite character?" with about 5 different characters to choose from. More specific feedback is meant to be given on the feedback page.

Comic Directory: This page will be a list of every comic that is currently on the website in order of comic number. I would like to add a few bells and whistles to make finding comics easier. These would include: small thumbnail images for each comic, buttons to filter by series/subseries, and sorting options.

Comic Content Page: Every comic will have its own dedicated page that follow a similar format. Each content page will have the comic's title, the comic itself, unique Brawl in the Family style mouse-over text, a description, a button to go back to the comic directory, and buttons to go to other comics. The buttons will bring readers to: the first comic, the previous comic, the next comic, and the latest comic.

Art Directory: This page will be very similar to the comic directory, but it will just show art pieces. My art pieces are not numbered, so I would like to put them in a grid view rather than a list. The art would be split up into different sections including main-series art, friendart, and style switch-ups. Since I have significantly less art pieces than I do comics, I would like to give each art piece a preview image.

Art Content Page: Following the same theme as the directory page, this page will also be similar to the comic content page. I don't think these pages would undergo a large rework like the directory page did, but will moreso be a watered down comic content page since the art pieces don't have as much going on as the comics. These pages will still have the title, drawing, and description, but will not have mouse-over text nor an intricate button system like the comic page had since my drawings are more self-contained There will still be buttons to go to other drawings, but it will likely just be next and previous buttons. I would like to give these buttons thumbnails for the next drawing so that readers know what drawing they will be going to. Each page will also have a "back to directory" button.

Feedback Page: I'd like to collect all kinds of feedback on this page with a focus on criticism. The poll from the home page will be there alonside a dedicated feedback form. The form will have an option to specify which series the feedback is aimed at. There will be a small message above the feedback form where I politely ask for criticism. Additionally, I could include another poll-like question to be answered in the feedback form and past poll questions with results.
